ArticleCrux under intense attack from three different sources
AuthorPeter Newport
Published19 February 2026
PrinciplesPrinciple 1 (Accuracy, Fairness and Balance); Principle 4 (Comment and Fact)

1. Summary

This complaint concerns an article published by Crux News on 19 February 2026, labelled “Editorial By Peter Newport.” The article contains a specific, verifiable false statement of fact about an identifiable LinkedIn post and its comments, directly contradicted by documentary evidence. Beyond this primary breach, the article presents contested allegations about named individuals and organisations without evidence, balance, or right of reply. It breaches Principle 1 (Accuracy, Fairness and Balance) and Principle 4 (Comment and Fact).

2. Principle 1 — Accuracy, Fairness and Balance

Breach 1 — Specific false statement of fact, verifiable by evidence (Primary Breach)

Newport states that Richard Thomas’s LinkedIn post generated “a tidal wave of abusive comments from many other key business leaders” and that “the post and the comments were removed by LinkedIn.” Both claims are demonstrably false. The post was not removed. The comments were not abusive — they were professional and measured expressions of support from identifiable senior business figures, including a partner at Russell McVeagh, the CEO of Glenorchy Air, and an Independent Planning Consultant. The only threatening language in the thread came from Newport himself, who twice commented that “Mr Thomas should perhaps exercise caution.” This is not a matter of editorial interpretation or opinion — it is a false statement of verifiable fact, documented by screenshots.

Breach 2 — No right of reply

The article makes serious negative characterisations of at least two QLDC councillors, the ODT/Mountain Scene, and Destination Queenstown. There is no indication any were approached for comment before publication.

Breach 3 — Unsubstantiated claim of coordinated attack

The article’s central premise — that three separate groups are mounting a coordinated “intense attack” on Crux — is presented as fact. No evidence of coordination between these groups is provided.

Breach 4 — Serious allegation of editorial corruption without evidence

The claim that QLDC’s $200,000 payment to ODT/Mountain Scene influences that outlet’s editorial decisions is a serious allegation. No evidence is offered and no response from the ODT is included.

Breach 5 — Self-admitted bias without correction

Newport acknowledges Crux is “guilty of letting our frustration come through in our coverage on occasion,” yet the article itself demonstrates this failing without any remediation or separation from factual content.

3. Principle 4 — Comment and Fact

Breach 1 — Assertion presented as fact

“Council staff are, Crux understands, scared of potential job losses and changes in policy.” No source is identified. “Crux understands” is used as attribution without any basis being provided.

Breach 2 — Personal inference presented as established fact

“Our council pours $200,000 into the ODT/Mountain Scene who back local business and council interests, to the exclusion of important local issues — no doubt to encourage the advertising revenue they depend on.” The phrase “no doubt” reveals this is Newport’s personal conclusion, not a reported fact.

Breach 3 — Subjective personal experience presented as reportage

Newport describes a councillor’s “snake hiss of pure contempt” and another who made him feel as though he was “not quite poking me in the chest.” These are subjective personal interpretations presented as if they are reportable events.

Breach 4 — Insinuation without evidence

“My home security system has strangely gone offline, new Facebook pages are being launched to produce ‘news media scrutiny’.” The word “strangely” implies a connection to the alleged attacks without any evidence of one.

4. Conclusion

The primary breach in this article is the publication of specific, verifiable false statements of fact about an identifiable event — claims directly contradicted by documentary evidence the complainant holds. This goes beyond the recurring pattern of blurred opinion and fact, and represents a straightforward failure of basic factual accuracy. The complainant will provide screenshot evidence of the LinkedIn thread with this complaint.

  • Principle 1 (Accuracy, Fairness and Balance): The article contains a specific, verifiable false statement of fact about a LinkedIn post and its comments, presents unsubstantiated allegations against named parties without right of reply, and makes a serious allegation of editorial corruption without evidence.
  • Principle 4 (Comment and Fact): The article systematically presents personal inference, subjective experience, and unsourced assertion as reportable fact, without clear labelling or distinction from factual content.
